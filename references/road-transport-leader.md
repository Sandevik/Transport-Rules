## What is a Vägtransportledare?

A **vägtransportledare** (road transport leader/escort) is a specially trained and licensed person who provides escort and guidance for **oversized or overweight transports** (tunga/breda/långa transporter) on public roads in Sweden.

Their role is to:
1. Plan and lead the transport safely through the route
2. Warn other road users of the oversized load
3. Communicate with the transport driver and, when necessary, with police
4. Ensure the transport complies with permit conditions

---

## When is a Vägtransportledare Required?

A road transport leader is typically required when a transport **exceeds standard dimensions or weights** and has been granted a special transport permit (dispensansökan). Specific thresholds that trigger the escort requirement:

| Dimension | Threshold requiring escort |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| Width | Over **3.5 m** (varies by road and permit) |
| Length | Certain long combination vehicles and exceptional loads |
| Weight | Exceptional weights beyond BK1 limits with special dispensation |

The exact requirement depends on the **dispensation permit** (special transport permit) issued by Trafikverket or the local road authority. The permit specifies:
- Whether a vägtransportledare is required
- How many escorts are required (front, rear, or both)
- Any police escort requirements
- Permitted time windows and route restrictions

## Licensing and Qualification (Utbildning och legitimation)

To work as a vägtransportledare, a person must:

1. **Complete approved training** — a certified training course covering:
   - Swedish traffic legislation
   - Rules for exceptional transport (dispenstransporter)
   - Communication procedures
   - Safety protocols
   - Use of escort vehicle equipment

2. **Hold a valid legitimation** (credential) issued by **Transportstyrelsen**

3. **Drive an approved escort vehicle** equipped with required warning equipment:
   - Yellow warning lights (rotating/flashing)
   - Signs indicating oversized load
   - Radio communication equipment

## Duties and Responsibilities (Uppgifter och ansvar)

The vägtransportledare is responsible for:

| Duty | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Route inspection | Verify the route is passable for the load before transport |
| Advance notification | Notify relevant authorities and Trafikverket |
| Lead/follow escort | Position escort vehicle correctly (front or rear) |
| Traffic management | Warn oncoming traffic, manage narrow passages |
| Permit compliance | Ensure transport stays within permit conditions |
| Emergency response | Coordinate response if incident occurs |

The transport company bears overall legal responsibility, but the vägtransportledare is personally responsible for proper execution of escort duties.

---

## Special Transport Permits (Dispensansökan)

Transports exceeding standard limits require a **special permit** from:
- **Trafikverket** — for state roads (riksvägar, europavägar)
- **Municipal road authority** — for municipal roads
- Both, if the route crosses both road categories

The permit application must include:
- Vehicle specifications (dimensions and weights)
- Proposed route
- Dates and time windows
- Any escort and police requirements

**Contact:** Trafikverket, dispensavdelningen (exceptions department)

---

## Interaction with Police Escort

For very large or heavy transports, a **police escort** (poliseskortering) may be required in addition to, or instead of, a vägtransportledare. The dispensation permit will specify this requirement.

Police escort is typically required when:
- Width exceeds approximately 5+ metres
- The load creates significant traffic disruption
- Special hazards exist on the route

---

## Penalties

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|------------|
| Conducting oversized transport without required escort | Sanction fee |
| Vägtransportledare without valid legitimation | Criminal offence |
| Exceeding permit conditions (route, time, dimensions) | Sanction fee + potential permit revocation |
