## Overview

**Miljöbalken (SFS 1998:808)** — Sweden's Environmental Code — is the primary framework law for environmental protection. For transport operators, it establishes obligations regarding:
- Emissions and air quality
- Noise
- Hazardous substances and fuel storage
- Reporting and permits for environmentally hazardous activity
- Sanctions for violations

While much day-to-day transport environmental compliance is handled by EU regulations (Euro standards, CO2 targets — see `eu-emissions.md`), Miljöbalken provides the Swedish legal framework and enforcement mechanism.

## Key Chapters for Transport

### Chapter 2 — General Rules of Consideration (Allmänna hänsynsregler)

Every operator must:
- Apply **best available technology (BAT)** to minimise environmental impact (2 kap. 3 §)
- Choose the **least harmful alternative** if several options exist (2 kap. 4 §)
- **Stop activities** that cause significant environmental damage (2 kap. 9 §)

These principles apply to transport companies operating vehicle fleets, fuel storage, and maintenance facilities.

### Chapter 9 — Environmentally Hazardous Activity (Miljöfarlig verksamhet)

Large vehicle depots, fuel stations, and maintenance workshops may qualify as **miljöfarlig verksamhet** requiring:
- **Permit (tillstånd)** from Länsstyrelsen or Mark- och miljödomstolen — for larger facilities
- **Registration (anmälan)** — for smaller facilities listed in Miljöprövningsförordningen (SFS 2013:251)
- Ongoing reporting and inspection obligations

**Thresholds for transport facilities (indicative — check current Miljöprövningsförordningen):**
- Fuel storage > 1 000 000 litres: permit required
- Fuel storage 10 000–1 000 000 litres: registration (anmälan) to municipality
- Vehicle washing facilities above certain capacity: registration
- Workshops with certain chemical handling: registration

### Chapter 26 — Supervision (Tillsyn)

Tillsynsmyndigheter (supervisory authorities) — municipalities for smaller activities, Länsstyrelsen for larger — can:
- Inspect facilities
- Issue compliance orders (förelägganden) with fines (viten)
- Order activities to cease

For transport-relevant environmental matters, the **municipality** (kommunen) typically has primary tillsynsansvar.

### Chapter 29 — Criminal Provisions (Straffbestämmelser)

Environmental crimes relevant to transport:
- **29 kap. 1 §** — Miljöbrott: intentional or grossly negligent serious environmental damage → imprisonment up to 2 years
- **29 kap. 2 §** — Grovt miljöbrott: serious/aggravated environmental crime → imprisonment 6 months to 6 years
- **29 kap. 4 §** — Försvårande av miljökontroll: obstruction of environmental control
- **29 kap. 11 §** — Otillåten miljöverksamhet: operating without required permit/registration

### Chapter 30 — Environmental Sanctions (Miljösanktionsavgifter)

**Miljösanktionsavgifter** are administrative fees (not criminal penalties) imposed for specific regulatory violations. They are:
- Fixed amounts set in **Förordning (2012:259) om miljösanktionsavgifter**
- Imposed by the supervisory authority
- **Not dependent on intent or negligence** — strict liability
- Concurrent with, not a substitute for, criminal prosecution

**Transport-relevant examples:**
| Violation | Fee range |
|-----------|----------|
| Starting miljöfarlig verksamhet without registration | 5 000 – 50 000 SEK |
| Not reporting to tillsynsmyndigheten as required | 1 000 – 50 000 SEK |
| Failure to perform required environmental measurement | 5 000 – 100 000 SEK |

## Emission and Noise Exemptions

### Noise exemptions
Transport operations (road haulage) can apply to the municipality for:
- **Dispensation from noise limits** during specific operations (e.g. night deliveries to hospitals, construction logistics)
- Based on 2 kap. and 9 kap. Miljöbalken

### Emission exemptions
Sweden can grant temporary national exemptions from Euro emission standard requirements under:
- 9 kap. Miljöbalken (through government regulation)
- EU framework directives
These are rare and require government decision.

## Low-Emission Zones (Miljözoner)

Swedish municipalities can establish **miljözoner** (low-emission zones) under:
- **Lag om miljözoner (SFS 1997:1336)** (referenced via Trafikförordningen)
- Classes: Miljözon 1, 2, 3 (tightening restrictions)

Current zones exist in Stockholm, Gothenburg, and Malmö (central areas).

| Zone class | Restriction |
|-----------|------------|
| Miljözon 1 | No pre-Euro III diesel trucks during zone hours |
| Miljözon 2 | More restrictive — typically Euro VI or electric only |
| Miljözon 3 | Most restrictive — zero-emission vehicles only |

Zones and their hours vary by city. Violations result in a **felparkeringsavgift** (fixed fine) or traffic penalty.

## Fuel and Hazardous Substance Storage

Operators storing diesel or AdBlue at depots must comply with:
- **Förordning (1998:901) om verksamhetsutövares egenkontroll** — self-monitoring and record-keeping
- Municipality fuel storage regulations
- Naturvårdsverket guidelines on fuel oil and chemical storage near water
