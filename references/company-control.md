# Företagskontroll av kör- och vilotider — Company Control of Driving and Rest Times

**Last verified: 2026-03-19**

## Overview

Transport companies (transportföretag) have a legal obligation under EU Regulation 561/2006 **Article 10** to organise driver schedules in a manner that makes compliance with driving and rest time rules possible. Merely having rules in place is insufficient — the company must actively verify and document compliance.

**Transportstyrelsen** has been the responsible authority for company inspections since 2011.

---

## EU Quantitative Inspection Requirements

Sweden must inspect **3% of all working days** performed by drivers subject to driving/rest time regulations.

Transportstyrelsen and Polismyndigheten share responsibility: **each inspects 50%** of required days — half via company inspections (företagskontroller), half via roadside checks (vägkontroller).

## Company Obligations

### Planning and Organisation
- Routes and schedules must be planned to allow drivers to comply with all kör- och vilotidsregler
- Companies may not offer financial incentives (bonuses, pay structures) that reward drivers for exceeding driving limits or taking insufficient rest
- Timetables and work schedules must be realistic given the distances and conditions involved
- Companies must plan routes so drivers can return home or to the operational centre at least **every four weeks** (Mobility Package 2022)

### Monitoring
Companies must periodically check that drivers comply with the rules by analysing tachograph data:
- **Digital tachograph data** from driver cards and vehicle units must be downloaded and checked
- **Vehicle unit download interval:** At least every **90 days**
- **Driver card download interval:** At least every **28 days**
- Data must be stored for at least **12 months** after the date of download
- TachoScan Control (analytical software) is used by Transportstyrelsen inspectors

### Documentation
- All tachograph data, logbooks, and attestation forms must be kept accessible
- Companies must be able to produce records during both **roadside checks** and **company inspections**
- An e-service is available for bus and goods companies: "Buss- och godsföretag – kontroll av kör- och vilotider samt vägarbetstid"

## Company Inspection Process (Företagskontroll)

Inspections examine a **two-month period** and typically cover the entire company's transport operations:
- Transportstyrelsen examines whether the company has properly planned routes and monitored compliance
- All drivers' records for the two-month period are typically reviewed

**Company selection criteria (risk-based assessment):**
- Results from police roadside checks
- Previous company inspection findings (valid for 2 years in risk assessment)
- Information from other authorities
- Reports from other EU member states via ERRU (European Register of Road Transport Undertakings)
- Random selection or sector/regional targeting

---

## Inspection Methods

Compliance is checked through two channels:

| Method | Who conducts it |
|--------|----------------|
| Roadside checks (vägkontroller) | Police (Polismyndigheten) |
| Company inspections (företagskontroll) | Transportstyrelsen |

Both national and international controls occur. Inspectors check:
- Tachograph records (färdskrivare)
- Record sheets (diagramblad) for analog tachographs
- Driver cards (förarkort)
- Attestation forms for non-driving periods
- **Lookback period:** 56 days (since 31 December 2024, extended from previous 28 days)

## Warning System (from 1 January 2026)

A new enforcement approach allows **warnings (varning)** to replace sanction fees in appropriate cases:

**When a warning may be issued (instead of sanction fee):**
- First-time or minor violation
- Company demonstrates willingness to correct the deficiency
- No systematic breach pattern
- No safety risk or unsuitable working conditions

**When a warning is NOT available:**
- Previous warnings or penalties exist for similar violations
- Systematic violations are present
- Large-scale or serious breaches
- Safety risk or seriously inadequate working conditions exist

**Who decides:** Only **Transportstyrelsen** may decide whether a warning is sufficient. Police may still issue advance penalty payments (förskottsbetalning) at roadside checks. If Transportstyrelsen later determines a warning is appropriate, advance payments are refunded.

## Sanctions for Companies

### Documentation Failure (Failure to Produce Records)

| Instance | Fee per vehicle | Condition |
|----------|----------------|-----------|
| First instance | **20,000 SEK** | — |
| Second instance | **40,000 SEK** | Within 3 years of first |
| Third instance | **60,000 SEK** | — |
| Maximum cap | **800,000 SEK** | Total across all vehicles |

### Other Company Violations

| Violation | Fee |
|-----------|-----|
| Cabotage violation | **60,000 SEK** |
| Missing required transport documents | **10,000 SEK** per violation |
| Failing to notify traffic manager | **10,000 SEK** |
| Failing to report employee numbers (annual, deadline 31 March) | **6,000 SEK** |
| Vehicle registration violation | **6,000 SEK** |

### Traffic License Consequences

Inspection results may trigger:
- License supervision (tillsyn av trafiktillstånd)
- Reassessment of operator suitability
- Repeated documentation failures can prevent future license issuance

## Beställaransvar — Orderer Responsibility

Any party that **orders (beställer)** transport services also bears responsibility:
- Must know or have reasonable grounds to assume the transport company is properly licensed
- Must verify the transport provider's authorization when contracting directly
- Applies to anyone who has agreed to transport "in any capacity"

**Penalties for orderers:**
- Domestic unlicensed transport: fines or **imprisonment up to 1 year**
- International transport violations: **fines only**

A free online lookup tool and the fee-based BKY (Behörighetskontroll Yrkestrafik) service are available for verification:
https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/bestallaransvar/

---

## Supervision Fees (Tillsynsavgift)

Transportstyrelsen funds inspection activities through supervision fees paid by transport companies. The fee is collected separately from sanction fees.
