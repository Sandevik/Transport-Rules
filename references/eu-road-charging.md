## Overview

The **Eurovignette Directive** (1999/62/EC as amended by 2022/362/EU) governs how EU member states may charge heavy goods vehicles for use of road infrastructure. The 2022 revision is a major overhaul:
- Mandates transition from **time-based** (vignettes) to **distance-based** tolls
- Requires **CO2 differentiation** of toll rates
- Introduces mandatory **air quality surcharges** in polluted areas

---

## Key Deadline: Time-Based → Distance-Based

| Milestone | Date | Requirement |
|-----------|------|------------|
| CO2 toll differentiation mandatory | **25 March 2024** | All existing distance-based toll systems must vary rates by CO2 emissions |
| Air quality charge option | **25 March 2022** | Member states may introduce surcharge in areas exceeding air quality limits |
| Air quality charge mandatory (if applicable) | **25 March 2026** | Member states with polluted areas must introduce the charge |
| Time-based vignettes abolished for HGV | **25 March 2030** | No pure time-based vignettes for vehicles > 3.5t on TEN-T network |

After **2030**, all new charging on the TEN-T network for HGV must be distance-based.

---

## CO2 Toll Differentiation (from March 2024)

Member states with distance-based tolls must vary the infrastructure charge based on CO2 emission class:

| CO2 class | Variation vs reference |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Zero-emission (electric/hydrogen) | **-100%** (no infrastructure charge) |
| Ultra-low emission | Up to **-75%** |
| Euro VI or equivalent | Reference rate (0%) |
| Below Euro VI | **Up to +100%** surcharge |

This means a Euro III truck may pay **double** the toll of a Euro VI truck on the same route.

---

## Air Quality Surcharge (from March 2026)

Member states whose road network areas exceed EU air quality limit values must apply an additional **air quality surcharge** on top of the infrastructure charge:
- Applied during peak hours in affected urban/suburban areas
- Rate: up to average infrastructure charge × 1.5
- Revenue must be used for air quality improvement measures

---

## External Cost Charge (Optional)

Member states may additionally charge for **external costs** (noise, air pollution, CO2):
- Not mandatory, but permitted
- Rate capped based on marginal social cost calculations
- Must be used for transport sector or environmental purposes

---

## Eurovignette Countries

Sweden does **not** currently operate a Eurovignette system for HGV — no motorway tolls on the main road network (except the Öresund Bridge and a few urban tunnels/bridges). However:
- Swedish operators travelling to **Germany, France, Belgium, Netherlands, Luxembourg, Denmark** encounter:
  - Germany: **Maut** — distance-based HGV toll (LKW-Maut), recently extended to 3.5+ t vehicles
  - France: **Péage** — motorway tolls
  - Belgium: **Viapass** — km-based HGV charge
  - Austria: **ASFINAG Maut** — motorway toll

---

## European Electronic Toll Service (EETS)

**Directive 2019/520/EU** establishes EETS — a single OBU (on-board unit) that works across all EU toll systems. Swedish operators can use a single EETS provider for:
- German Maut
- Austrian ASFINAG
- Belgian Viapass
- Other EETS-compliant systems

EETS providers registered in Sweden: check Transportstyrelsen's EETS register.

---

## HGV Vehicles Subject to Directive

The Eurovignette Directive applies to vehicles:
- **>3.5 tonnes** GVW (expanded from >12t by 2022/362/EU amendments)
- Used for road freight transport
- On the TEN-T network (and member states may extend to other roads)

---

## Sweden-Specific: Congestion Charges

Stockholm and Gothenburg operate **trängselskatt** (congestion charges) under Swedish national law — not the Eurovignette system. These apply to most vehicles including private cars, but HGV are also subject. See `tax-procedures.md` for details.
