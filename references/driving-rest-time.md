## Scope — Who Must Follow These Rules

EU Regulation 561/2006 applies to:
- Vehicles (including trailers/semi-trailers) with combined total weight exceeding **3.5 tonnes**
- All **buses** regardless of weight
- From **1 July 2026**: international goods transport with vehicles weighing **2.5–3.5 tonnes** (Mobility Package)

Light vehicles (≤3.5 tonnes) used in **domestic** Swedish transport follow separate rules — see `light-vehicles.md`.

**Three main objectives of the rules:**
1. Ensure fair competition between road transport sector operators
2. Provide drivers with good social conditions
3. Increase traffic safety

---

## Daily Driving Time (Körtid per dygn) — Article 6

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Normal daily driving | **9 hours** |
| Extended daily driving (max 2×/week) | **10 hours** |

- The 9-hour limit applies within any 24-hour period starting from the end of the last weekly rest.
- Extension to 10 hours is allowed at most **twice per calendar week**.
- "Calendar week" runs Monday to Sunday.

---

## Weekly Driving Time (Körtid per vecka) — Article 6

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Maximum per week | **56 hours** |
| Maximum over any 2 consecutive weeks | **90 hours** |

---

## Breaks / Raster — Article 7

After **4 hours 30 minutes** of driving, a mandatory break must be taken **before** continuing:

| Option | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Single break | At least **45 minutes** |
| Split break | First: ≥ **15 minutes** + Second: ≥ **30 minutes** (in that order only) |

- Breaks may not be spent on other work.
- They interrupt the 4h30min accumulation — the clock resets after a valid break.
- The second part of a split break must be at least 30 minutes; reversed order (30+15) is NOT valid.

---

## Daily Rest (Dygnsvila) — Article 8

| Type | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Regular daily rest | **11 consecutive hours** within each 24-hour period |
| Reduced daily rest | **9 consecutive hours** (allowed max **3 times** between weekly rests) — no compensation required |
| Split daily rest | First period: ≥ 3 hours + Second period: ≥ 9 hours = **minimum 12 hours total** |

- The 24-hour period starts from the end of the previous daily or weekly rest.
- The 3-hour reduction (from 11 to 9 hours) may be used maximum 3 times between any two weekly rest periods.
- No compensation needed for the reduced (9h) daily rest.

---

## Weekly Rest (Veckovila) — Article 8 & 9

| Type | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Normal weekly rest | **45 consecutive hours** |
| Reduced weekly rest | Minimum **24 consecutive hours** |
| Over 2 consecutive weeks | Must include at least **one normal (45h) weekly rest** |

**Compensation for reduced weekly rest:**
- The shortfall (45h minus actual rest taken) must be compensated by attaching it to a rest period of ≥9h
- Must be compensated **within 3 weeks** of the reduced rest week
- The compensation rest may not be taken in the vehicle cab (Mobility Package 2022 rule)

**Cab prohibition (from 21 February 2022):** Drivers may NOT take regular weekly rest (≥45h) in the vehicle cab. Employers must provide suitable accommodation (hotel, etc.) at own cost when driver is away from home base. Reduced weekly rest (24h) may be taken in the cab.

**Return home requirement (Mobility Package):** Companies must plan routes enabling drivers to return home or to the company's operational centre at least **every four weeks**.

---

## Multi-Crew Operations (Flermansbetjäning)

When two drivers share a vehicle:
- Each driver is entitled to a daily rest period within any **30-hour period** (not 24h)
- First driver may drive for maximum **1 hour** before the second driver joins
- Minimum 9 hours rest required for each within the 30-hour period
- Both drivers must be present in the vehicle throughout (but non-driving driver counts as on break)

---

## Exceptional Circumstances Extensions (Article 12)

In exceptional circumstances (emergencies, unforeseen events), a driver may extend daily or weekly driving time:
- By **up to 1 hour** to reach the employer's operational centre or driver's home for weekly rest
- By **up to 2 hours** (after a 30-minute break) to reach the centre/home for **regular weekly rest** only
- Driver must note the reason for the extension on the tachograph or attached sheet
- Applicable only when road safety is not compromised

---

## Coach/Bus Special Rules (Occasional International Passenger Services)

Drivers of coaches on single occasional international passenger services may:
- Postpone weekly rest for **up to 12 days** (instead of 6 weeks normal)
- Required compensation: the deferred rest must be taken immediately after the 12-day period
- Special break rules: breaks may be split into 15-minute increments (flexibility for passenger stops)

---

## AETR Rules (International Transport Outside EU/EEA)

AETR (European Agreement Concerning the Work of Crews of Vehicles Engaged in International Road Transport) applies to transport routes that pass through **non-EU/non-EEA countries** that have signed AETR (e.g., Turkey, Russia, Belarus, Morocco, Kazakhstan).

- AETR rules are substantially **identical to EU Regulation 561/2006** after alignment in 2010
- When a journey passes through both EU and AETR countries, **AETR applies for the entire journey**
- Key thresholds: same as EU rules (9h daily, 56h weekly, 90h over 2 weeks, 45-min break after 4h30min)
- **Advance payment at roadside: NOT permitted for AETR violations** (unlike EU regulation violations)
- Countries where AETR applies: all 56 signatory countries including Russia, Turkey, Ukraine, Belarus, Morocco, Kazakhstan, Azerbaijan, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan

## Tachograph (Färdskrivare) Requirements

**Who must have a tachograph:**
- All vehicles subject to EU 561/2006 (>3.5t and buses)
- From July 1, 2026: 2.5–3.5t vehicles in international transport

**Smart Tachograph version 2 (Gen 2) retrofit deadlines (Mobility Package):**
- New heavy vehicles: mandatory from **21 August 2023**
- Heavy vehicles with analogue/digital tachograph in international operations: retrofit by **31 December 2024**
- Heavy vehicles with Smart v1: upgrade to v2 by **18 August 2025**
- Light vehicles (2.5–3.5t) international: tachograph required by **1 July 2026** (must be Smart v2)

**Lookback period at roadside checks:** 56 days (changed from 28 days effective 31 December 2024)
- Drivers must be able to produce: driver card, tachograph printouts, attestation forms covering the last 56 days

**Driver card (förarkort):** Each driver must have an individual driver card. Only accredited workshops (ackrediterade verkstäder) may install and calibrate tachographs.

**Records without tachograph:** When a driver has been absent (illness, holiday, etc.) and could not use the tachograph, they must carry a completed attestation form (Intyg om aktiviteter) for those days.

## Penalties for Violations

From **1 January 2026**, a new warning system applies:
- Transport companies may receive a **warning** instead of a sanction fee for minor first-time violations
- Warnings are NOT available if: previous violations exist, systematic breaches occur, safety risks are present
- Police may still issue **advance payments** at roadside checks; refunds issued if Transportstyrelsen later issues a warning instead
- For AETR transport: no advance payment permitted

For specific SEK amounts, see `sanctions-penalties.md`.
