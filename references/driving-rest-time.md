**Last verified:** March 2026

## Scope — Who Must Follow These Rules

EU Regulation 561/2006 applies to:
- Vehicles (including trailers/semi-trailers) with combined total weight exceeding **3.5 tonnes**
- All **buses** regardless of weight
- From **1 July 2026**: international goods transport with vehicles weighing **2.5–3.5 tonnes** (Mobility Package)

Light vehicles (≤3.5 tonnes) used in **domestic** Swedish transport follow separate rules — see `light-vehicles.md`.

---

## Daily Driving Time (Körtid per dygn) — Article 6

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Normal daily driving | **9 hours** |
| Extended daily driving (max 2×/week) | **10 hours** |

- The 9-hour limit applies within any 24-hour period starting from the end of the last weekly rest.
- Extension to 10 hours is allowed at most **twice per calendar week**.

---

## Weekly Driving Time (Körtid per vecka) — Article 6

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Maximum per week | **56 hours** |
| Maximum over any 2 consecutive weeks | **90 hours** |

---

## Breaks / Raster — Article 7

After **4 hours 30 minutes** of driving, a mandatory break must be taken **before** continuing:

| Option | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Single break | At least **45 minutes** |
| Split break | First: ≥ **15 minutes** + Second: ≥ **30 minutes** (in that order) |

- Breaks may not be spent on other work.
- They interrupt the 4h30min accumulation.

---

## Daily Rest (Dygnsvila) — Article 8

| Type | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Regular daily rest | **11 consecutive hours** within each 24-hour period |
| Reduced daily rest | **9 consecutive hours** (allowed max **3 times** between weekly rests) — no compensation required |
| Split daily rest | 3 hours + 9 hours = **12 hours total** (first period ≥3h, second ≥9h consecutive) |

- The 24-hour period starts from the end of the previous daily or weekly rest.
- Multi-manning (two drivers): each driver entitled to daily rest; the 30-hour period applies for multi-manned vehicles.

---

## Weekly Rest (Veckovila) — Article 8 & 9

| Type | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Normal weekly rest | **45 consecutive hours** |
| Reduced weekly rest | Minimum **24 consecutive hours** |
| Over 2 consecutive weeks | Must include at least **one normal (45h) weekly rest** |

**Compensation for reduced weekly rest:**
- The difference (between 45h and what was taken) must be attached to a rest period of ≥9h
- Must be compensated **within 3 weeks** of the reduced rest week
- Compensation may not be taken in the vehicle cab (since Mobility Package 2022)

**Cab prohibition:** Drivers may not take regular weekly rest (≥45h) in the vehicle cab. Employers must provide suitable accommodation (hotel etc.) when the driver is away from home base.

## AETR Rules (International Transport Outside EU/EEA)

Source: https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/kor-och-vilotider/

AETR (European Agreement Concerning the Work of Crews of Vehicles Engaged in International Road Transport) applies to transport routes that pass through **non-EU/non-EEA countries** that have signed AETR (e.g., Turkey, Russia, Belarus).

- AETR rules are substantially **identical to EU Regulation 561/2006** after alignment in 2010
- When a journey passes through both EU and AETR countries, **AETR applies for the entire journey**
- Key thresholds: same as EU rules (9h daily, 56h weekly, 90h over 2 weeks, 45-min break after 4h30min)

## Tachograph (Färdskrivare) Requirements

Source: https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/kor-och-vilotider/

**Who must have a tachograph:**
- All vehicles subject to EU 561/2006 (>3.5t and buses)
- From July 1, 2026: 2.5–3.5t vehicles in international transport

**Smart Tachograph version 2 (Gen 2) requirements (Mobility Package):**
- New heavy vehicles: mandatory from **21 August 2023**
- Heavy vehicles in international operations: must retrofit by **31 December 2024**
- Version 2 specifically: by **18 August 2025**
- Light vehicles (2.5–3.5t) international: by **1 July 2026**

**Driver card (förarkort):** Each driver must have an individual driver card. Only accredited workshops (ackrediterade verkstäder) may install and calibrate tachographs.

**Records without tachograph:** When a driver has been absent (illness, holiday, etc.) and could not use the tachograph, they must carry a completed attestation form (Intyg om aktiviteter).

## Penalties for Violations

From **1 January 2026**, a new warning system applies:
- Transport companies may receive a **warning** instead of a sanction fee for minor first-time violations
- Warnings are NOT available if: previous violations exist, systematic breaches occur, safety risks are present
- Police may still issue **advance payments** at roadside checks; refunds issued if Transportstyrelsen later issues a warning instead

For specific SEK amounts, see `sanctions-penalties.md`.
