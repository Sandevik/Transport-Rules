## Overview

**Regulation (EC) No 1/2005** sets EU-wide rules for the transport of live vertebrate animals in connection with economic activity. It covers:
- All species of vertebrate animals transported commercially
- All stages of transport including loading, transit, and unloading
- Both domestic and international transport within/between member states

The regulation is directly applicable in all member states (no national transposition needed for the core rules).

---

## Scope

### Applies to:
- Transport of live vertebrate animals in connection with **economic activity**
- Transport operators, drivers, attendants, dealers, and markets
- Transport within and between EU member states

### Does NOT apply to:
- Transport by farmers of their own animals using their own vehicles (for farming purposes) for short journeys
- Transport of pets with owners (non-commercial)
- Registered equidae (horses) going to/from veterinary treatment

---

## Key Requirements

### 1. Authorisation of transporters

**Short journeys (≤8 hours):** Transporter must hold a **Type 1 authorisation** from Jordbruksverket.
**Long journeys (>8 hours):** Transporter must hold a **Type 2 authorisation** — more stringent requirements.

Both types valid for 5 years, issued by the national competent authority.

**Swedish authority:** Jordbruksverket (Swedish Board of Agriculture)

### 2. Driver and attendant certificate

Drivers and attendants of animals must hold a **certificate of competence** covering:
- Animal physiology, behaviour, and welfare needs
- Handling and loading/unloading techniques
- Emergency care
- Record keeping

Certificate issued by approved training bodies, recognised across EU.

### 3. Means of transport requirements

Vehicles must:
- Have adequate ventilation (adjustable for temperature and stocking density)
- Have non-slip floors
- Have drainage where appropriate
- Have adequate space (see stocking densities below)
- Be equipped to provide water and feed
- For long journeys: GPS tracking and automated climate control

### 4. Fitness for transport

Animals must be **fit for transport**. Animals that are:
- Sick or injured
- Heavily pregnant (within 10% of gestation for long journeys)
- Newborn (umbilical cord not yet healed)

...must NOT be transported (or only with veterinary certificate under specific conditions).

## Journey Time Limits

### Solipeds (horses, donkeys, mules), cattle, sheep, goats, pigs

#### Pigs
| Stage | Duration |
|-------|---------|
| Maximum journey | **24 hours** (with water available at all times) |
| After 24 hours | 24-hour rest + water + feed before continuing |

#### Cattle, sheep, goats (unweaned young)
| Stage | Duration |
|-------|---------|
| Travel | **9 hours** |
| Rest | **1 hour** (water + if necessary, feed) |
| Further travel | **9 hours** |

#### Cattle, sheep, goats (adults)
| Stage | Duration |
|-------|---------|
| Long journey maximum | **29 hours** travel time |
| After 29 hours | 24-hour rest required at approved station |

#### Horses (registered equidae for competition etc.)
- Up to **24 hours** without rest if watered every 8 hours and fed

#### Poultry, domestic birds, domestic rabbits
- Maximum **12 hours** without rest (then 24 hours rest, water, feed)

|--------------|-----------------|
| ~100 kg | 0.235 m² |
| ~200 kg | 0.36 m² |
| >100 kg (sows) | 0.40 m² |

### Cattle (indicative)

## Documentation for Long Journeys (>8 hours)

A **journey log** must be prepared and approved by the competent authority before departure. The journey log covers:

1. **Place of departure** and intended place of destination
2. **Estimated journey times** and rest points
3. **Veterinary certificates** (for cross-border transport)
4. **Vehicle** details and transporter authorisation number
5. **Animals:** species, number, age, weight (where relevant)

Journey log signed off at:
- Departure
- Each checking point / rest stop
- Destination

---

## Temperature Rules

During transport, temperature inside the vehicle must be maintained:
- **Between 5°C and 30°C** (±5°C tolerance)
- For long journeys: automatic climate control with alarm required

If temperature exceeds limits: journey must be interrupted and animals rested.

---

## Enforcement in Sweden

**Länsstyrelsen** conducts controls at loading points, markets, and during transport. **Polismyndigheten** may conduct roadside checks.

**Penalties for violations (Swedish law, via Jordbruksverket framework):**
- Revocation of transporter authorisation
- Administrative fines
- Criminal prosecution for serious animal welfare violations (Djurskyddslagen SFS 2018:1192)
