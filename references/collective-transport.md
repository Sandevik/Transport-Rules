## Definition of Collective Transport (Kollektivtrafik)

Under EU Regulation 1370/2007, Article 2a, collective transport means:

"Persontransporter av allmänt ekonomiskt intresse som erbjuds allmänheten fortlöpande och utan diskriminering"

(Person transport of general economic interest offered to the public on an ongoing and non-discriminatory basis.)

### What Is NOT Collective Transport

The following are explicitly excluded:

| Excluded service type | Reason |
|----------------------|--------|
| Charter and demand-responsive services | Not offered to the general public |
| Circular routes returning to starting point | Not point-to-point |
| Event-specific transport | Not continuous |
| Tourism packages bundling accommodation | Transport not primary purpose |
| Services where transport is secondary | Transport is incidental |

## Regulatory Authority

**Transportstyrelsen** (Swedish Transport Agency) is responsible for:
1. Issuing regulations governing collective transport operators
2. Conducting compliance oversight of **regional collective transport authorities (RKM)** and operators
3. Monitoring market conditions and competition

**Regional Collective Transport Authorities (Regionala kollektivtrafikmyndigheter — RKM):**
- Each Swedish county has an RKM responsible for planning and ensuring adequate public transport
- RKMs operate under the Collective Transport Act (2010:1065)

---

## Permit Requirements

Commercial collective transport operators must obtain **authorization** and comply with notification requirements.

Operators must notify Transportstyrelsen when they:
- **Commence** new collective transport services
- **Continue** services beyond a certain period
- **Cease** services

**Notification requirements governed by:** TSFS 2012:2

## Types of Collective Transport Services

### Linjetrafik (Scheduled Line Services)
- Fixed routes with published timetables
- Open to all passengers
- Requires linjetrafiktillstånd (line traffic permit)
- May be subject to public service obligations (PSO) under EU 1370/2007

**Subpage:** https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/kollektivtrafik/

### Behovstrafik (Demand-Responsive Transport)
- Transport organized based on passenger demand
- May include special transport for disabled persons (färdtjänst, riksfärdtjänst)
- Different regulatory requirements from fixed-line services

## Traffic Supply Programs

Regional authorities must produce **traffic supply programs** (trafikförsörjningsprogram) that:
- Define the public's needs for transport in the region
- Specify baseline service levels
- Are renewed periodically

Transportstyrelsen inspects compliance with these programs.

---

## Passenger Rights and Universal Accessibility

Operators must comply with:
- **Accessibility standards** (tillgänglighetskrav) — vehicles and stops must be accessible to persons with disabilities
- **Passenger information** — clear and timely information about timetables, routes, delays, and rights
- **Non-discrimination** — equal access to all members of the public

---

## Supplementary Charges (Tilläggsavgift)

Under SFS 1977:67, operators may impose a **reasonable supplementary charge** on passengers who:
- Do not hold a valid ticket
- Cannot provide proof of entitlement

The charge may cover:
- The applicable fare
- Reasonable costs for ticket verification and administration

Applying supplementary charges requires **approval from Transportstyrelsen** (apply via vag@transportstyrelsen.se).

---

## Public Service Obligations (PSO)

Under EU Regulation 1370/2007, regional authorities may:
- **Grant exclusive rights** to operators for specific routes
- Provide **public service compensation** (finansiering) for services that would not be commercially viable
- Enter into **public service contracts** (avtal om allmän trafik)

Operators receiving PSO contracts must comply with the conditions including service frequency, vehicle standards, and pricing.

---

## Oversight and Inspection

Transportstyrelsen's inspectors (including Samer Sada, as referenced on the agency website) verify:
- Traffic supply program compliance
- Universal accessibility standards
- Notification obligations
- Passenger information transparency
- Compliance with service contracts
