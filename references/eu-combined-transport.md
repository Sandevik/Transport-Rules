# EU Combined Transport — Directive 92/106/EEC

**Sources:**
- Directive 92/106/EEC — on the establishment of common rules for certain types of combined transport of goods between Member States

- Swedish implementation: Lag (2012:210) om kombinerade transporter

**Last verified:** March 2026

---

## What is Combined Transport?

**Combined transport** means a freight journey where the **long-haul leg** is carried by rail, inland waterway, or sea — and the **initial and/or terminal legs** are by road. This reduces road congestion and emissions.

**Directive 92/106/EEC** provides:
1. Exemption from certain **driving bans** on the road legs
2. **Weight allowance** (44 t instead of the standard 40 t road limit)
3. Tax advantages (member state option)

---

## Definition of Combined Transport (Art. 1)

A transport is "combined" under the directive when:

1. **Road leg:** The vehicle (lorry, trailer, swap-body, or container on a semi-trailer) travels by road **to or from** a rail terminal, port, or inland waterway terminal
2. **Non-road leg:** The main leg is at least **100 km** by rail, inland waterway, or maritime route
3. **International:** The journey crosses at least **one EU member state border**

The road legs (initial and terminal) must be:
- Within the **same member state** as departure or destination
- Or to the **nearest suitable** rail/water terminal for the goods

---

## Key Benefits for Operators

### 1. Extended weight limit — 44 tonnes

Road vehicles carrying a container or swap-body as part of a combined transport journey may exceed the standard 40 t EU limit up to **44 tonnes**, provided the vehicle has 5+ axles.

This applies on the road legs of the combined journey.

### 2. Exemption from driving bans

The road legs of a combined transport journey are **exempt from national driving bans** (restrictions on HGV driving during weekends, public holidays, night hours) that would otherwise apply.

In Sweden: HGV are generally not subject to weekend driving bans (unlike some other EU states), but this exemption is relevant for operations in Germany, France, Italy where bans exist.

### 3. Tax advantages (optional for member states)

Member states **may** provide tax relief (fuel tax, vehicle tax, tolls) for transport operators using combined transport. Sweden has limited specific incentives beyond the weight allowance.

---

## Documentation Required

For the driving ban exemption and weight allowance, operators must carry:

- **Confirmation document** issued by the rail/port/waterway operator confirming:
  - The non-road leg booking or completion
  - Route, distance, and terminal details
  - Dates of transport
- **CMR waybill** or equivalent for the road leg
- Transport contract details

---

## Road Leg Distance Limits

The directive does not set explicit maximum distances for road legs, but requires road legs to be the "shortest reasonable route" to/from the terminal. In practice:

- Road legs should not exceed **~150 km** (rough guidance, not codified)
- Enforcement: if road leg is disproportionately long, combined transport status may be denied

---

## 2023 Revision Proposal

The European Commission proposed a significant revision in **September 2023 (COM/2023/0525)**:
- Extend to **domestic** combined transport (removing the cross-border requirement)
- Include **short sea shipping** more explicitly
- Better definition of eligible road-leg distances
- Digital documentation requirements
- Status: Under Council and Parliament negotiation as of 2026

---

## Sweden's Implementation

Sweden implements the directive through **Lag (2012:210) om kombinerade transporter**. Key points:
- Applies to international combined transport as per directive
- The 44 t weight allowance applies on Swedish roads for qualifying combined transport
- No specific Swedish weekend driving ban to be exempted from (Sweden does not have general HGV weekend bans unlike Germany/France)

---

## Practical Example

A Swedish company ships containers from Gothenburg to Duisburg (Germany) by ship (360 km sea route), then by road to final destination in Munich (550 km road):
- **Sea leg = non-road leg** (> 100 km): qualifies
- **Road leg in Germany:** Exempt from German weekend driving ban
- **Weight:** Up to 44 t on 5+ axle vehicle in Germany
- **Documentation:** Gothenburg port + Duisburg port confirmation document required in cab
