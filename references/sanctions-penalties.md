## Overview

Sanctions in Swedish commercial transport are administered by **Transportstyrelsen** (Swedish Transport Agency) and enforced by **Polismyndigheten** (Police) and **Tullverket** (Customs) at roadside checks.

From **1 January 2026**, a new warning system allows companies to receive a **warning** instead of a sanction fee for minor first-time violations.

---

## Sanctions for Transport Companies (Transportföretag)

### Road Control Violations

| Violation | Fee (SEK) |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Cabotage violation** (illegal domestic transport by foreign carrier, EU Reg. 1072/2009) | **60,000** |
| **Posting of drivers violation** — foreign company lacks valid posting declaration at roadside check (only during cabotage) | **60,000** (advance payment at roadside) |
| **Missing required transport documents** — Community license, driver attestation, line traffic permit, control document, CEMT permit, bilateral/special permit | **10,000** per violation |
| **Vehicle registration violation** — using vehicles not properly registered on the professional license, or failing to deregister unused vehicles | **6,000** |
| **Foreign vehicle registration violation** — non-Swedish registered vehicles lacking required conditions | **7,500** |

### Supervision/Inspection Violations

| Violation | Fee (SEK) |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Failing to report employee numbers** (annual deadline: 31 March each year) | **6,000** |
| **Failing to notify about responsible traffic manager** | **10,000** |
| **Non-compliance with tested persons reporting requirements** | **10,000** |

### Failure to Submit Documentation (Company Inspections)

When a company fails to submit adequate documentation during company inspection (företagskontroll):

| Instance | Fee (per vehicle) |
|----------|-----------------|
| First instance | **20,000 SEK** |
| Second instance (within 3 years) | **40,000 SEK** |
| Third instance | **60,000 SEK** |
| Maximum cap (all vehicles combined) | **800,000 SEK** |

## Sanctions for Drivers (Förare)

Drivers can receive sanction fees for violations including those committed **outside Sweden**. The principle: "sanktionsavgift påförs även den förare som har begått överträdelser utanför Sverige" (fees apply even for violations committed abroad).

Violation categories for drivers include:
- Exceeding daily driving time limits (over 9 hours or 10-hour extension without entitlement)
- Insufficient daily rest periods (below 11 hours regular, or abusing 9-hour reduction more than 3×/week)
- Insufficient weekly rest (below 45 hours normal or 24 hours reduced)
- Insufficient breaks (driving more than 4.5 hours without a 45-minute break)
- Tachograph card violations — driving without inserting driver card, using another person's card
- Missing or incorrectly completed attestation forms (intyg om aktiviteter)
- AETR violations (no advance payment permitted for AETR operations)

## Warning System (from 1 January 2026)

From 1 January 2026, transport companies may receive a **warning (varning)** instead of a sanction fee when:
- First-time or minor violation
- No prior warnings or penalties for similar violations within recent period
- No systematic breach pattern
- No safety risk or seriously inadequate working conditions
- Warning is deemed sufficient given the severity of the violation

Warnings are **NOT available** when:
- Previous violations or warnings for similar offences exist
- Systematic violations are present
- Large-scale or serious breaches
- Safety risk or seriously inadequate working conditions exist

**Who decides:** Only **Transportstyrelsen** may issue a warning. Police conduct roadside checks and may collect advance payments, but cannot issue formal warnings — that authority rests with Transportstyrelsen.

---

## Advance Payments at Roadside Checks (Förskottsbetalning)

Foreign-based companies and drivers may be required to pay a **cash advance (säkerhet)** at roadside inspections:
- Advance is collected to secure eventual penalty payment
- Full determination is later made by Transportstyrelsen
- If Transportstyrelsen later issues a warning instead of a fee, the advance is **refunded**
- Police (Polismyndigheten) and Customs (Tullverket) have authority to collect advances at roadside
- **AETR operations:** No advance payment permitted

## Överlastavgift — Overload Fee (SFS 1972:435)

### What Triggers an Overload Fee

An overload fee (överlastavgift) applies when a vehicle's **actual weight exceeds the permitted maximum** for:
- Total weight (totalvikt / bruttovikt)
- Axle load (axeltryck)
- Bogie load (boggitryck)
- Triple axle load (trippelaxeltryck)

### Who Pays

The overload fee is imposed on the **vehicle's owner (ägaren)**, not necessarily the driver, unless the driver is also the owner.

### Fee Calculation

The overload fee is calculated based on the **degree of overload** as a percentage of the permitted limit. The fee schedule under SFS 1972:435 is graduated:

| Overload above limit | Fee basis |
|---------------------|-----------|
| 0–2% | Minimum base fee (lower tier) |
| 2–5% | Graduated — increases with excess |
| 5–10% | Significantly higher graduated fee |
| 10–20% | High graduated fee |
| Over 20% | Maximum tier — can reach tens of thousands of SEK |

The exact fee schedule is set by government ordinance and is subject to annual adjustment. For current amounts, verify with Transportstyrelsen or the full text of SFS 1972:435 and its associated ordinance.

As a practical reference: fees can reach **tens of thousands of SEK** for serious overloads (e.g., exceeding axle limits by 20%+).

**Legal basis:** SFS 1972:435 — Lag om överlastavgift
Full text: https://www.riksdagen.se/sv/dokument-och-lagar/dokument/svensk-forfattningssamling/lag-19721435-om-overlastavgift_sfs-1972-435/

### Inspection Process

- Police (Polismyndigheten) or Transportstyrelsen inspectors may weigh vehicles at roadside
- Mobile weighing equipment (both static and dynamic/in-motion weighing)
- Fixed weigh stations also exist on major routes
- Overloaded vehicles may be required to **offload excess cargo** before continuing
- Vehicle may be immobilised until compliant

## Criminal Penalties

Some violations carry criminal (not administrative) sanctions:

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Operating without taxi driver credential (taxiförarlegitimation) | Criminal — fines |
| Operating without traffic permit (trafiktillstånd) — domestic | Fines or **imprisonment up to 1 year** |
| International transport violations (operating without permit / cabotage breach) | Fines only |
| Ordering transport from unlicensed operator — domestic | Fines or **imprisonment up to 1 year** |
| Ordering transport from unlicensed operator — international | Fines only |
| Tachograph manipulation (installing/using manipulation device) | Criminal prosecution |
| Using another person's driver card | Criminal offence |

---

## Working Time Violations (under SFS 2005:395)

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Employer exceeding permitted weekly working hours | Fines |
| Unauthorized overtime per hour per worker | 1% of the annual price base amount per hour |
| Failure to maintain working time records | Fines |
| Failure to provide required breaks | Fines |
| Providing false information to Transportstyrelsen | Fines |
| Violation of Board directives | Up to **1 year imprisonment** or fines |

---

## Enforcement Bodies

| Body | Role |
|------|------|
| Transportstyrelsen | Issues sanction fee decisions; company inspections; warning decisions; appeals authority |
| Polismyndigheten | Roadside checks; advance payment collection; criminal enforcement |
| Tullverket | Roadside checks for non-Swedish vehicles; advance payment collection |
| Åklagarmyndigheten / Domstol | Criminal prosecution for serious violations |
