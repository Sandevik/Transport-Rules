## Overview

Sanctions in Swedish commercial transport are administered by **Transportstyrelsen** (Swedish Transport Agency) and enforced by **Polismyndigheten** (Police) at roadside checks.

From **1 January 2026**, a new warning system allows companies to receive a **warning** instead of a sanction fee for minor first-time violations.

---

## Sanctions for Transport Companies (Transportföretag)

| Violation | Fee (SEK) |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Cabotage violation** (illegal domestic transport by foreign carrier) | **60,000** |
| **Posting of drivers violation** — foreign company lacks valid posting declarations | **60,000** (advance payment at roadside) |
| **Missing required transport documents** | **10,000** per violation |
| **Vehicle registration violation** — using unregistered vehicles or failing to deregister | **6,000** |
| **Foreign vehicle registration violation** — registering non-Swedish vehicles incorrectly | **7,500** |
| **Failing to report employee numbers** (annual, deadline 31 March) | **6,000** |
| **Failing to notify about responsible traffic manager** | **10,000** |
| **Non-compliance with tested persons reporting requirements** | **10,000** |

### Warning System (from 1 January 2026)

Warnings may replace sanction fees when:
- First-time or minor violation
- No prior warnings or penalties for similar violations
- No systematic breach pattern
- No safety risk or seriously inadequate working conditions

Warnings are NOT available for systematic violations, large-scale breaches, or safety-critical issues.

---

## Sanctions for Drivers (Förare)

Drivers can receive sanction fees for violations occurring **outside Sweden**. The principle: "sanktionsavgift påförs även den förare som har begått överträdelser utanför Sverige" (fees apply even for violations committed abroad).

**Specific driver sanction amounts** are set in the regulations governing kör- och vilotider. Fetch the current schedule from:
https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/sanktionsavgifter-inom-yrkestrafiken-pa-vag/

Typical violation categories for drivers include:
- Exceeding daily driving time limits
- Insufficient daily rest periods
- Insufficient weekly rest
- Insufficient breaks (raster)
- Tachograph card violations
- Missing or incorrectly completed attestation forms

## Advance Payments at Roadside Checks (Förskottsbetalning)

Foreign-based companies and drivers may be required to pay a **cash advance** (säkerhet) at roadside inspections:
- Advance is collected to secure eventual penalty payment
- If Transportstyrelsen later issues a warning instead of a fee, the advance is **refunded**
- Police have authority to collect advances at roadside; only Transportstyrelsen makes the final determination

## Överlastavgift — Overload Fee (SFS 1972:435)

### What Triggers an Overload Fee

An overload fee (överlastavgift) applies when a vehicle's **actual weight exceeds the permitted maximum** for:
- Total weight (totalvikt / bruttovikt)
- Axle load (axeltryck)
- Bogie load (boggitryck)
- Triple axle load (trippelaxeltryck)

### Who Pays

The overload fee is imposed on the **vehicle's owner** (ägaren), not necessarily the driver, unless the driver is also the owner.

### Fee Calculation

The overload fee is calculated based on the **degree of overload** as a percentage of the permitted limit:

| Overload (% above limit) | Fee |
|--------------------------|-----|
| Up to 5% | Lower fixed fee |
| 5–10% | Graduated fee |
| Over 10% | Higher graduated fee (significant amounts) |

The exact fee schedule is set by the government (government ordinance). As a general reference, fees can reach tens of thousands of SEK for serious overloads.

**Source:** SFS 1972:435 — Lag om överlastavgift

### Inspection Process

- Police (Polismyndigheten) or Transportstyrelsen inspectors may weigh vehicles at roadside
- Mobile weighing equipment is used
- Both static (fixed weigh stations) and dynamic (in-motion) weighing may be used
- Overloaded vehicles may be required to **offload** excess cargo before continuing

## Criminal Penalties

Some violations carry criminal (not administrative) sanctions:

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Operating without taxi driver credential (taxiförarlegitimation) | Criminal — fines |
| Operating without traffic permit (trafiktillstånd) — domestic | Fines or **imprisonment up to 1 year** |
| International transport violations (operating without permit / cabotage breach) | Fines only |
| Ordering transport from unlicensed operator — domestic | Fines or **imprisonment up to 1 year** |
| Ordering transport from unlicensed operator — international | Fines only |

## Enforcement Bodies

| Body | Role |
|------|------|
| Transportstyrelsen | Issues sanction fee decisions; company inspections; appeals authority |
| Polismyndigheten | Roadside checks; advance payment collection; criminal enforcement |
| Åklagarmyndigheten / Domstol | Criminal prosecution for serious violations |
