## What is ADR?

**ADR** (Accord relatif au transport international des marchandises Dangereuses par Route) is the international agreement governing road transport of dangerous goods. It is updated every two years (ADR 2025 applies from 1 January 2025, with a 6-month transition for the 2023 version until 30 June 2025).

**Directive 2008/68/EC** makes ADR mandatory within the EU for both international and domestic transport. In Sweden, MSB administers ADR via MSBFS regulations.

---

## Scope

ADR applies to transport of:
- **Explosives** (Class 1)
- **Gases** (Class 2) — compressed, liquefied, dissolved
- **Flammable liquids** (Class 3) — including petrol, diesel above thresholds
- **Flammable solids** (Class 4.1, 4.2, 4.3)
- **Oxidising substances and organic peroxides** (Class 5.1, 5.2)
- **Toxic and infectious substances** (Class 6.1, 6.2)
- **Radioactive materials** (Class 7)
- **Corrosive substances** (Class 8)
- **Miscellaneous dangerous substances** (Class 9) — incl. lithium batteries, dry ice

**Exemptions** apply for small quantities (LQ — limited quantities), quantities below certain thresholds, and transport by private individuals.

## UN Numbers and Proper Shipping Names

Every dangerous good has a **UN number** (e.g. UN 1203 = petrol/gasoline, UN 1978 = propane) and a **Proper Shipping Name** (PSN). These determine:
- Hazard class and packing group
- Packaging requirements
- Labelling/placarding
- Documentation requirements
- Transport restrictions

## Key ADR Requirements

### 1. Driver training and ADR certificate

Drivers transporting dangerous goods (above LQ and small load thresholds) must hold a valid **ADR certificate** (ADR-intyg/ADR-bevis):
- **Basic certificate** — all road transport of dangerous goods
- **Specialisation for tanks** — required for tank vehicle transport
- **Specialisation for explosives (Class 1)**
- **Specialisation for radioactive (Class 7)**

**Training:** Approved ADR training course + theoretical exam
**Certificate validity:** 5 years (renewable with refresher training)
**Issuing authority in Sweden:** Transportstyrelsen (via approved training centres)

### 2. Vehicle and equipment requirements

- Correct **placards** (orange panels, hazard diamond labels) on all four sides of vehicle/tank
- **Fire extinguishers** (sizes per ADR 8.1.4)
- **Warning triangle** and appropriate PPE (gloves, eye protection)
- **Written instructions** (Schriftliche Weisungen / skriftliga instruktioner) — one set per language of the countries being traversed
- Appropriate **ADR marking** on vehicle

### 3. Packaging

- Approved packaging (UN-marked) required for most goods
- Packaging tested and certified according to ADR Chapter 6
- Segregation requirements between incompatible goods (Table 7.2.4)

### 4. Transport documents

Required documents in the cab:
- **Transport document** (fraktsedel/transportdokument) — must include UN number, PSN, hazard class, packing group, quantity
- **ADR certificate** (driver's)
- **Vehicle approval certificate** (for tank vehicles)
- **Written instructions** (emergency action document)

### 5. Tunnels

Tunnel restrictions per ADR Chapter 8.6:
- Tunnels classified A (no restriction) through E (prohibition)
- Dangerous goods vehicles may be restricted from certain tunnels
- Alternative routes must be planned

## Transport Categories and Threshold Quantities

ADR defines **transport categories 0–4** for substances. When a vehicle carries mixed goods, the total transport index determines whether full ADR requirements apply:

| Transport category | Threshold for full ADR compliance |
|-------------------|----------------------------------|
| 0 | Always full ADR |
| 1 | > 20 (in mixed load calculation) |
| 2 | > 333 |
| 3 | > 1000 |
| 4 | Unlimited quantity — not restricted |

Below thresholds: **exemptions apply** (still requires some markings and documentation).

## Safety Adviser (Säkerhetsrådgivare / DGSA)

Companies that transport, pack, load, or unload dangerous goods must appoint a **Dangerous Goods Safety Adviser (DGSA/säkerhetsrådgivare)** unless fully exempt:
- Holds a DGSA certificate (written exam)
- Responsible for monitoring compliance
- Submits annual report on company's dangerous goods activities
- Must report accidents involving dangerous goods to MSB

**Legal basis:** ADR 1.8.3, implemented via MSBFS

---

## Swedish Enforcement

- **Polisen** — roadside checks of ADR compliance (driver certificate, documents, vehicle equipment)
- **MSB** — regulatory authority, sanctions for systemic non-compliance
- **Transportstyrelsen** — vehicle approval certificates for tank vehicles

**Penalties in Sweden:**
- Driving without valid ADR certificate: böter (fine) or körkortsåterkallelse
- Serious violations: up to imprisonment under Swedish criminal law
- Administrative sanctions: MSB can impose fines for systemic failures

---

## ADR 2025 Key Changes (vs ADR 2023)

- Updated provisions for **lithium batteries** (Class 9) — tightened rules for damaged/defective batteries
- New requirements for **electric vehicles** during transport
- Updated **special provisions** for certain Class 3 substances
- Revised **packaging instructions** for several UN numbers
