## Overview

Two parallel EU emission frameworks apply to heavy commercial vehicles:
1. **CO2 fleet targets** — average fleet CO2 emissions per km (manufacturer obligation)
2. **Euro emission standards** (Euro I–VII) — pollutant limits (NOx, PM, HC) for individual vehicles at type approval

Both affect which vehicles operators can buy/use and the tax/charge advantages/disadvantages they face.

## CO2 Fleet Targets — Regulation EU 2024/1610

### Target structure (% reduction vs 2019 reference)

| Deadline | Reduction target | Applies to |
|---------|----------------|-----------|
| 2025 | **-15%** | Manufacturers' new HDV fleet average CO2 |
| 2030 | **-45%** | Manufacturers' new HDV fleet average CO2 |
| 2035 | **-65%** | Manufacturers' new HDV fleet average CO2 |
| 2040 | **-90%** | Manufacturers' new HDV fleet average CO2 |

**Note:** These targets bind **vehicle manufacturers**, not transport operators directly. However, they drive the market toward zero-emission vehicles, affecting:
- Availability of diesel trucks
- Purchase prices of ZEV vs diesel
- Potential future restrictions on fossil fuel vehicles

### Vehicles covered (Regulation 2024/1610)

- **Heavy-duty trucks** (lorries M3, N2, N3 > 3.5t)
- **City buses** (from 2030)
- **Long-distance coaches** (from 2030)
- **Urban delivery vehicles** (N1/N2 from 2030)

### Zero-emission vehicle (ZEV) mandate

From 2030: At least **35%** of new heavy trucks sold must be zero-emission (ZEV).
From 2035: At least **70%** must be ZEV.

### Impact on operators

- **2030:** Significant share of new truck market will be BEV (battery electric) or FCEV (fuel cell hydrogen)
- **2040:** Effectively only ZEV and very few special-purpose ICE vehicles can be newly registered
- Operators planning fleet renewal should factor in charging/refuelling infrastructure and total cost of ownership for ZEV

## Euro VII — Regulation EU 2024/1257

### Introduction dates

| Vehicle type | Euro VII applies from |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Heavy-duty vehicles (N2, N3, M2, M3) | **1 July 2026** (new type approvals); **1 July 2027** (all new registrations) |
| Light-duty vehicles (N1, M1) | Separate timeline (later) |

### Key Euro VII pollutant limits (vs Euro VI)

Euro VII tightens limits for:
- **NOx (Nitrogen Oxides):** Further reduction from Euro VI (already strict)
- **PM (Particulate Matter):** Lower particle count limits, including ultrafine particles
- **PN (Particle Number):** Stricter number limits
- **NH3 (Ammonia):** New limit for the first time
- **N2O (Nitrous oxide):** New limit for the first time

### New testing conditions (Euro VII)

- **Extended on-road testing** (RDE — Real Driving Emissions) — tighter conformity factors
- **In-service conformity testing** throughout vehicle lifetime (not just at type approval)
- **Brake emission limits** — for the first time, limits on non-exhaust emissions (brake dust, tyre wear)
- **Battery durability** — for hybrid/electric powertrains

### Implications for operators

- Euro VII trucks from 2026 will have more advanced aftertreatment systems (SCR, EGR, DPF)
- Increased AdBlue (DEF/SCR fluid) consumption in some configurations
- Higher new vehicle purchase prices due to stricter tech requirements

## Euro Emission Classes and Current Vehicles

### Current Euro standards (Heavy Duty, as of 2026)

| Standard | Introduced | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Euro I | 1992 | Very old fleet, few remaining |
| Euro II | 1996 | Old fleet |
| Euro III | 2000 | Many older vehicles |
| Euro IV | 2005 | |
| Euro V | 2008 | Common in fleet |
| **Euro VI** | **2013** | **Current standard for new registrations until 2026** |
| **Euro VII** | **2026** | **Next standard** |

### Practical significance for operators

- **Miljözoner** — low emission zones in Swedish cities restrict pre-Euro III or pre-Euro VI vehicles
- **Insurance and tax incentives** — Euro VI/VII vehicles may qualify for reduced vehicle tax
- **Resale value** — older Euro class = lower resale

## Bonus-Malus for Heavy Vehicles

Sweden's bonus-malus system (passenger cars primarily) is being extended/adapted for commercial vehicles. Current status:
- **Supermiljöbilspremie** — grant for purchase of ZEV trucks (check current availability with Naturvårdsverket/Transportstyrelsen)
- **Reduktionsplikt** — obligation on fuel suppliers to blend increasing proportions of renewable fuel into diesel/petrol. Drivers see this as higher fuel prices for fossil diesel (indirect incentive for HVO)
