## Definition of Taxi Traffic

"Taxitrafik är trafik som bedrivs med personbil eller lätt lastbil och som innebär att fordon och förare mot betalning ställs till allmänhetens förfogande för transport av personer."

Translation: Taxi traffic is traffic conducted with a passenger car or light truck where vehicle and driver are made available to the general public for payment for transporting persons.

## Taxi Driver Requirements (Taxiförarlegitimation)

All taxi drivers must hold a valid **taxiförarlegitimation** (taxi driver credential/license) issued by Transportstyrelsen.

**To qualify, a driver must:**
- Hold a valid Swedish driving license (at minimum class B)
- Pass a knowledge test covering local geography, legislation, and professional conduct
- Meet health requirements (medical fitness)
- Have a clean criminal record (inga allvarliga brott)
- Be at least 21 years old

**Supervision:**
Transportstyrelsen supervises taxi drivers regarding:
- Health conditions (hälsa)
- Criminal history (brottsbelastning)
- Conduct issues (lämplighet)

Drivers who no longer meet the requirements may have their credential revoked.

**Subpage:** https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/taxi/taxiforarlegitamation/

## Taxi Company Permit (Taxitrafiktillstånd / Trafiktillstånd)

All taxi operators must hold a **trafiktillstånd** (traffic permit) issued by Transportstyrelsen.

**Requirements for a traffic permit (per EU 1071/2009 and SFS 2012:1156):**
1. **Good repute (gott anseende):** No serious criminal convictions; tax compliance; no serious road traffic or commercial transport violations
2. **Financial standing (ekonomisk förmåga):** Sufficient financial resources (SEK 9,000 for first vehicle + SEK 5,000 for each additional)
3. **Professional competence (yrkeskunnande):** Must have a qualified traffic manager (trafikansvarig) who has passed the CPC knowledge examination
4. **Established in Sweden (etablering):** Company must be genuinely established in Sweden

**Subpage:** https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/taxi/trafiktillstand/

## Taxi Vehicle Requirements (Taxifordon)

- Vehicle must be a **passenger car or light truck**
- Must be registered for taxi use in the Swedish vehicle registry
- Must be equipped with an **approved taximeter** (taxameter) — see taxameter requirements
- Must display required markings (taxi sign/lykta) when in service
- Annual vehicle inspection requirements apply

**Taximeter (taxameter):** Taximeters must meet approved standards. Transportstyrelsen sets requirements for taximeter calibration and approval.

**Subpage:** https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/taxi/taxameter/

## Price Transparency (Prisinformation)

Taxi operators must provide clear price information to passengers:
- Prices must be displayed clearly before the journey
- Price list must be accessible in the vehicle
- Passengers must be able to obtain a receipt

**Subpages:**
- For operators: https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/taxi/prisinformation-/
- For customers: https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/taxi/prisinformation-taxi/

## Accounting Clearinghouses (Redovisningscentraler)

Taxi operators using electronic payment systems must register with an **approved accounting clearinghouse** (redovisningscentral). These clearinghouses collect transaction data to ensure tax compliance and oversight.

**Subpage:** https://www.transportstyrelsen.se/sv/vagtrafik/yrkestrafik/taxi/redovisningscentraler-for-taxi/

## Rest Time Rules for Taxi Drivers

Taxi drivers are covered by the **light vehicle rest time rules** (Förordning 1994:1297), NOT by EU Regulation 561/2006, since taxis use vehicles ≤3.5 tonnes.

Key requirements:
- At least **11 hours rest** per 24-hour period
- Personal logbook (personlig tidbok) must be maintained
- Both driver AND employer are responsible for compliance

See `light-vehicles.md` for full details.

## Penalties

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|------------|
| Operating without taxiförarlegitimation | Criminal offence — fines or imprisonment |
| Operating without trafiktillstånd | Criminal offence — fines or imprisonment up to 1 year |
| Operating without approved taximeter | Sanction fee |
| Failure to provide price information | Sanction fee |
| Failure to register with clearinghouse | Sanction fee |
