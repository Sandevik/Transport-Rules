## Overview

For transport companies, four main tax regimes are relevant:

1. **F-skatt / Arbetsgivardeklaration** (Skatteförfarandelagen) — corporate/employer tax compliance
2. **Energiskatt** (fuel tax) — tax on diesel and other fuels
3. **Vägtrafikskatt** (vehicle tax) — annual road tax per vehicle
4. **Trängselskatt** (congestion charge) — in Stockholm and Gothenburg

---

## 1. Skatteförfarandelagen (SFS 2011:1244) — Tax Procedure Act

The SFL governs how taxes are administered, reported, and collected in Sweden. Key provisions for transport companies:

### F-skatt (Corporate/Self-Employed Tax)

- Transport companies must hold **F-skattsedel** (F-tax approval)
- Signals to customers that the company handles its own tax and social fees
- Required for subcontractors — a customer using a subcontractor without F-skatt may be held liable for unpaid taxes
- Apply via Skatteverket

**Checking subcontractor F-skatt:** Always verify via Skatteverket before engaging a subcontractor: https://www.skatteverket.se/

### Arbetsgivardeklaration (Employer Declaration)

Transport companies with employees must file monthly:
- **Arbetsgivardeklaration på individnivå (AGI)** — monthly declaration listing each employee's salary and tax withheld
- Due: 12th of the following month (or next banking day)
- Filed via Skatteverket's e-service

Driver-specific considerations:
- Per diem allowances (traktamente) for overnight trips: **tax-free up to Skatteverket standard rates**
  - Swedish domestic overnight: 290 SEK/day (2025 rate — verify annually)
  - International: country-specific rates published by Skatteverket
- Milersättning (mileage allowance) for private car use on company business: 25 öre/km tax-free (2025 — verify)

### Documentation Obligations (SFL)

Under SFL, transport companies must retain:
- Accounting records: **7 years** after the end of the fiscal year
- Contracts, invoices, and receipts: 7 years
- Tachograph data and driver records: overlap with transport regulation requirements

## 2. Energiskatt — Fuel Tax (SFS 1994:1776)

### Diesel rates (indicative — subject to annual indexation)

| Fuel | Energiskatt | CO2-skatt | Total excl. VAT |
|------|------------|-----------|----------------|
| Diesel (standard) | ~2 100 SEK/m³ | ~2 800 SEK/m³ | ~4 900 SEK/m³ |
| HVO100 (renewable diesel) | Reduced/exempt | Reduced | Significantly lower |

**Note:** Exact rates change annually via government budget. Always verify current rates with Skatteverket or the Swedish Energy Markets Inspectorate.

### Tax refund for yrkestrafik (professional road transport)

Professional transport operators (yrkestrafik) may be eligible for:
- **Nedsättning av energiskatt** (reduced energy tax rate) for diesel used in professional road transport
- Applied via annual tax return
- Requires documentation of usage for qualifying transport

### Biofuel exemptions and reductions
- HVO (hydrotreated vegetable oil): reduced or zero CO2 tax depending on origin and certification
- FAME (biodiesel): partial tax reduction
- LNG/CNG: separate tax structure

## 3. Vägtrafikskattelagen — Vehicle Tax (SFS 2006:227)

Annual road tax (vägtrafikskatt) levied on registered vehicles based on:
- **Vehicle type and weight** (for trucks: chassis weight)
- **Fuel type** (diesel trucks pay higher base rate than petrol)
- **Euro emission class** (lower tax for cleaner vehicles)

### Truck tax bands (indicative — verify with Transportstyrelsen)

| Gross vehicle weight | Annual base (diesel) |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| 3 500 – 7 500 kg | ~4 000–8 000 SEK |
| 7 501 – 12 000 kg | ~8 000–15 000 SEK |
| > 12 000 kg | ~15 000–50 000+ SEK |

Euro VI vehicles receive a reduction compared to older Euro classes.

Tax is collected via direct debit (autogiro) or invoice from Transportstyrelsen.

### Suspension of tax (skatteavstängning)

Vehicles can be taken off road (avställda) — vehicle tax is suspended during avställning periods. Notification via Transportstyrelsen.

## 4. Trängselskatt — Congestion Charge (SFS 2004:629)

Applies in **Stockholm** and **Gothenburg** during weekday peak hours.

### Stockholm
- Charge points on routes in/out of the inner city
- Hours: 06:00–22:30 weekdays (no charge nights, weekends, public holidays)
- Fee per passage: **11–45 SEK** depending on time of day (max 135 SEK/day per vehicle)

### Gothenburg
- Similar structure, different charge points and rates
- Daily max: 60 SEK/vehicle

### Exemptions
- Vehicles registered abroad: **no congestion charge** (enforcement via camera + international agreement — being expanded)
- Emergency vehicles
- Military vehicles
- Buses in scheduled service (linjetrafik)
- Miljöfordon (green vehicles): exemptions are being phased out

### Payment
- Automatic via camera — Transportstyrelsen invoices registered keeper
- Foreign-registered vehicles: invoice sent to keeper in home country (under EU Directive 2015/413)
