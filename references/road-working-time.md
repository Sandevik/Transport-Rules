## Scope — Who Is Covered

The Road Working Time Act (SFS 2005:395) applies to:

- **Mobile workers** (förare and other crew) employed by companies in EU member states conducting road transport subject to EU Regulation 561/2006 or AETR
- **Self-employed drivers** conducting such transport
- Overseen by **Transportstyrelsen** (Swedish Transport Agency)

The law applies to vehicles subject to EU 561/2006 (>3.5t and buses). Drivers of light vehicles (≤3.5t) are **not** covered by this law but may be covered by general working time legislation.

---

## Definition of "Work"

Work encompasses the entire period from start to finish of the working day, including all of the following:

| Activity | Counted as work? |
|---------|-----------------|
| Driving | Yes |
| Loading and unloading | Yes |
| Assisting passengers | Yes |
| Vehicle cleaning and maintenance | Yes |
| Regulatory compliance documentation | Yes |
| Security oversight tasks | Yes |
| Waiting time driver cannot freely dispose of | Yes |
| Breaks (raster) | No |
| Daily rest (dygnsvila) | No |

---

## Maximum Working Hours

| Rule | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Ordinary weekly working time | Maximum **40 hours/week** |
| Permissible overtime / additional hours | Up to **200 hours/year** |
| Average weekly hours (over 4-month calculation period) | Maximum **48 hours/week** |
| Absolute weekly ceiling | **60 hours** in any single week |

- The 48-hour average is calculated over a rolling or fixed 4-month period
- No individual week may ever exceed 60 hours regardless of the averaging period

---

## Break Requirements (Raster under vägarbetstidslagen)

Breaks must be taken **before** continuing work after reaching the threshold:

| Continuous working time | Minimum break |
|------------------------|---------------|
| 6 hours | Break required |
| Between 6 and 9 hours | ≥ **30 minutes** |
| More than 9 hours | ≥ **45 minutes** |

**Split breaks:** Breaks may be divided into periods of **at least 15 minutes** each, taken at appropriate intervals during the shift.

**Interaction with 561/2006:** Driving breaks under EU 561/2006 (45 min after 4h30 driving) must also be observed. Where both sets of rules apply, the more restrictive applies.

---

## Night Work (Nattarbete)

**Definition of night:** Any part of the period **01:00–05:00**.

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|------------|
| Maximum night work | **10 hours** within any 24-hour period containing night work |
| Prerequisite | Must be preceded by an approved rest period |

---

## Calculation Period

- Standard calculation period: **4 months** (may be adjusted by collective agreement)
- Some collective agreements can extend the calculation period up to **6 months**
- Self-employed drivers: same 4-month calculation period applies

---

## Record-Keeping Obligations

Employers must:
- Maintain records of actual working hours per driver
- Records must be accessible to Transportstyrelsen inspectors
- Connected to tachograph data where applicable

---

## Enforcement and Penalties

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|------------|
| Employer violates working hour limits | **Fines** |
| Self-employed driver exceeds permitted hours | **Fines** |
| Failure to maintain records or advance notice obligations | **Fines** |

Police have the authority to monitor maximum weekly working hours at roadside checks and report violations to Transportstyrelsen.
