## Types of International Goods Transport

| Type (Swedish) | Type (English) | Definition |
|----------------|----------------|------------|
| Bilateral transport | Bilateral | Between two states; vehicle registered in one of them. Example: Swedish truck loads in Romania, unloads in Sweden |
| Transittrafik | Transit | Through a country without loading/unloading. Example: Russian truck passes through Sweden to Norway |
| Tredjeländestrafik | Third country | Between two states neither of which is the registration country. Example: Ukrainian truck from Norway to Sweden |
| Cabotagetrafik | Cabotage | Domestic transport within an EU/EEA country where the vehicle is not registered |

## Permit Requirements

### Community License (Gemenskapstillstånd)
Swedish transport companies conducting international goods transport within the EU/EEA must hold a **Community license** (gemenskapstillstånd).

- Issued by **Transportstyrelsen**
- Required for vehicles over **3.5 tonnes** conducting international professional transport
- From **21 May 2022** (Mobility Package): also required for **2.5–3.5 tonne** vehicles in international goods transport

### Certified True Copies
Each vehicle must carry a **certified true copy** (bestyrkt kopia) of the Community license in the vehicle at all times during international transport.

### Non-EU Permit Lists
For transport to countries outside the EU/EEA, special permits may be required depending on the destination country. Transportstyrelsen maintains a **permit list by country** (tillståndslista per land).

## Cabotage Rules (EU Regulation 1072/2009)

Cabotage = a foreign carrier performing **domestic transport within Sweden** (or another EU state).

### Mobility Package Rules (effective 21 February 2022)

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Max cabotage operations | **3 operations** within **7 days** following an international delivery |
| Cooling-off period | **4-day waiting period** between cabotage series (may not return to same member state) |
| Vehicle scope | Heavy vehicles >3.5t |
| Light vehicles (2.5–3.5t) | Subject to cabotage rules from **21 May 2022** |

**Combined transport restriction:** From 21 February 2022, combined transport (multi-modal with initial/final road leg in Sweden) is subject to the **same restrictions as cabotage** for foreign operators. Governed by SFS 1998:786 § 3f.

### Documents Required for Cabotage

The driver must carry in the vehicle:
- Valid Community license (certified true copy)
- Driver attestation (if driver is a non-EU national)
- Evidence of the international transport that preceded the cabotage (consignment notes/CMR)
- Evidence of each cabotage transport performed

### Penalty for Cabotage Violations

**60,000 SEK** sanction fee imposed on the transport company.

## Posting of Drivers (Utstationering)

When a foreign company sends drivers to perform transport within Sweden, **posting rules** apply (EU Directive 96/71/EG as amended by 2018/957/EU and Mobility Package 2020/1057):

- Posted drivers are entitled to Swedish minimum pay and working conditions
- The foreign company must file a **posting declaration** before the driver enters Sweden
- An **IMI number** (Internal Market Information system reference) is required
- If the declaration is missing at roadside check: **60,000 SEK advance payment**

From **2 February 2022**, posting rules apply to international road transport driving time in Sweden exceeding **3 days** in any **4-week period**.

## Annual Reporting Obligations

Companies conducting international operations with a consistent presence in Sweden must:
- **Register vehicles** in Sweden if conducting continuous operations here
- **Report employee numbers** annually by **31 March** each year
- Failure to report: **6,000 SEK** sanction fee

## Driver Nationality Rules for International Transport

| Driver nationality | International EEA transport (Swedish vehicle) |
|-------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| EEA citizen | Permitted |
| Non-EEA citizen | Permitted — but company must hold **driver authorization** (förartillstånd) |

The driver authorization (förartillstånd) is issued by Transportstyrelsen for non-EEA drivers employed by Swedish companies.

## Smart Tachograph Requirements (Mobility Package)

For vehicles conducting international transport:
| Deadline | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| 21 August 2023 | New heavy vehicles must have smart tachograph v2 |
| 31 December 2024 | Heavy vehicles in international operations must retrofit smart tachograph |
| 18 August 2025 | All heavy international vehicles: smart tachograph version 2 |
| 1 July 2026 | Light vehicles (2.5–3.5t) in international transport: tachograph required |
