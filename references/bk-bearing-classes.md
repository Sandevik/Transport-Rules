## Overview

Sweden classifies roads into four **bärighetsklasser (BK)** — load-bearing classes — that determine the maximum permissible axle loads, bogie loads, and gross vehicle weights. The class of a road is marked with signs, and Trafikverket publishes an interactive BK map.

Most state roads (statliga vägar) are BK1. Municipal and smaller forest roads are often BK2 or BK3.

**BK4** was introduced specifically to enable heavier Nordic timber and heavy-haul transport combinations.

---

## BK1 — Standard Roads

The default class for Sweden's main road network (riksvägar, europavägar, and most länsvägar).

### Axle loads (BK1)

| Axle type | Max load |
|-----------|---------|
| Driving axle (single) | **11.5 t** |
| Non-driving axle (single) | **10.0 t** |

### Bogie loads / Boggie (2 axles, BK1)

| Axle spacing | Max bogie load |
|-------------|---------------|
| < 1.0 m | 11 t |
| 1.0 – 1.3 m | 16 t |
| > 1.3 m | 18 t |

### Triple axle loads (BK1)

| Axle spacing | Max triple load |
|-------------|----------------|
| ≤ 1.3 m | 21 t |
| > 1.3 m | 24 t |

### Total vehicle weights (BK1)

| Vehicle configuration | Max gross weight |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| 2-axle lorry | 18 t |
| 3-axle lorry | 25 t |
| 4-axle lorry | 31 t (32 t with air suspension on all axles) |
| 4-axle articulated (semi-trailer) | 36 t |
| 5-axle articulated | 40 t |
| 6-axle articulated | 42 t |
| Road train (lastbil + släp), up to 7 axles | **64 t** |
| EMS/modular combinations (25.25 m) | **74 t** |

## BK2 — Reduced Capacity Roads

Applies to many county roads, municipal streets, and secondary roads. Signs must indicate BK2 status.

### Axle loads (BK2)

| Axle type | Max load |
|-----------|---------|
| Driving axle (single) | **10.0 t** |
| Non-driving axle (single) | **8.0 t** |

### Bogie loads (BK2)

| Axle spacing | Max bogie load |
|-------------|---------------|
| < 1.0 m | 10 t |
| 1.0 – 1.3 m | 13 t |
| > 1.3 m | 15 t |

### Triple axle loads (BK2)

| Axle spacing | Max triple load |
|-------------|----------------|
| ≤ 1.3 m | 18 t |
| > 1.3 m | 20 t |

### Total weights (BK2)

Maximum gross weights are proportionally reduced. The operator must calculate based on the axle/bogie limits above. No standard EMS combinations on BK2 roads.

---

## BK3 — Low-Capacity Roads

Applies to the weakest rural roads, forest roads, and private roads that are publicly accessible. Always verify before route planning.

### Axle loads (BK3)

| Axle type | Max load |
|-----------|---------|
| Single axle (all types) | **8.0 t** |

### Bogie loads (BK3)

| Axle spacing | Max bogie load |
|-------------|---------------|
| Any spacing | **11 t** |

### Triple axle loads (BK3)

| Axle spacing | Max triple load |
|-------------|----------------|
| Any spacing | **11 t** |

---

## BK4 — Heavy Nordic Roads

Introduced for the Swedish timber industry and bulk transport. BK4 roads allow higher total weight than BK1 for qualifying vehicle combinations, provided specific axle load limits are met.

**Key limit: Maximum gross weight = 74 tonnes** (same as BK1 EMS, but accessible on BK4-designated roads without needing EMS configuration).

### Axle loads (BK4)

| Axle type | Max load |
|-----------|---------|
| Driving axle (single) | **11.5 t** |
| Non-driving axle (single) | **10.0 t** |

BK4 uses the same *single-axle* limits as BK1 but enables higher *total weights* for multi-axle combinations.

### Total weight (BK4)

| Combination | Max gross weight |
|------------|----------------|
| Qualifying multi-axle timber/heavy transport | **74 t** |

**Conditions for BK4:**
- Vehicle must meet specific multi-axle requirements (typically 8+ axles for 74 t)
- Air suspension (or equivalent) on all driven and load-bearing axles
- Route must be designated BK4 — use Trafikverket's BK map to verify
- Speed restrictions apply (typically 80 km/h on BK4 roads for heavy combinations)

---

## Seasonal Restrictions — Tjällossning (Spring Thaw)

During spring thaw, road bearing capacity decreases. Many BK1 roads temporarily downgrade to BK2 or BK3. Typical period: **10 November – 10 April** (varies by region and year).

Trafikverket publishes real-time tjällossning restrictions on their website and via the BK map. Always check current restrictions before heavy transport.
