## Overview

When a transport company established in one EU member state sends drivers to perform work in another member state, the drivers may be **posted workers** — entitled to the host country's minimum working conditions. Directive 2020/1057/EU establishes specific rules for when road transport constitutes posting, addressing the unique nature of mobile transport work.

---

## The General Posting Framework

Under Directive 96/71/EC (as amended by 2018/957/EU), posted workers are entitled to:
- **Minimum wage** in the host member state
- **Maximum working time** and minimum rest periods
- **Equality of treatment** — no discrimination
- **Safe working conditions** per host country rules

The key question for transport operators: **when is a driver "posted"?**

---

## Directive 2020/1057/EU — Road Transport Specific Rules

### Bilateral transport — NOT posting

**Bilateral transport** between the country of establishment and another member state does **not** constitute posting. This means:
- A Swedish carrier sending a driver to pick up goods in Germany and return to Sweden: **no posting** → German minimum wage does not apply
- Similarly, transit through a third member state (without loading/unloading in the transit country) is **not** posting

### Cabotage = Posting

**Cabotage** (domestic transport within a foreign member state) is **always posting** from the first cabotage operation. The driver is entitled to:
- Host country minimum wage for the entire cabotage period
- Host country working time rules (where more protective)

**Cabotage rules (EU Reg. 1072/2009):** Maximum **3 cabotage operations within 7 days** after international delivery. Then a **cooldown period of 4 days** before returning to the same host country.

### Cross-trade transport — posting applies

**Cross-trade** (collecting goods in country A, delivering to country B, where the driver is established in country C): **posting applies** in both country A and country B.

### Transit — NOT posting

Driving through a member state without loading or unloading: **not posting** in the transit country.

## Practical Compliance — What Operators Must Do

### 1. Register the posting in advance

When drivers are posted to another EU member state, the employer must submit a **posting declaration** (IMI — Internal Market Information System or equivalent national portal) before the posting begins.

Each member state has its own portal:
- Germany: **FMS** (Fahrermeldestellen)
- France: **SIPSI**
- Netherlands: **DUOS**

### 2. Pay host country minimum wage

For cabotage and cross-trade operations, drivers must receive at least the **minimum wage** of the host country for the period spent there. This includes:
- Basic wage
- Applicable allowances and supplements

### 3. Keep documentation available

During cabotage/cross-trade operations, the driver must carry or have electronic access to:
- Proof of transport contract / CMR waybill
- Evidence of wages paid (or available in the cab via IMI)
- Working time records (tachograph)

### 4. Driver documentation in the cab

Per 2020/1057/EU, enforcement authorities (police) can require the driver to present:
- Proof of the nature of the transport (bilateral, cabotage, cross-trade)
- CMR waybills, consignment notes covering at least the previous 28 days
- Tachograph data (to verify the nature of operations)

---

## The 3-in-7 Cabotage Rule

After an international delivery to a member state:
- Up to **3 cabotage operations** permitted
- Within **7 days** from the date of the last unloading of the international load
- After the 7-day window, the vehicle must return with a new international load before further cabotage is permitted in that state

**Cooling-off period:** A vehicle that has performed the maximum cabotage in a member state cannot return to that state for further cabotage until **4 clear days** have elapsed.

## Swedish Minimum Wage (For Foreign Carriers in Sweden)

Sweden does not have a statutory minimum wage law. Instead, minimum wages are set by **collective agreements (kollektivavtal)**. For foreign carriers posting drivers to Sweden:
- The relevant collective agreement for road transport is **Transportavtalet** (between Transport Workers' Union/Transport and Swedish Transport Employers' Association)
- Foreign employers are not automatically bound by Transportavtalet unless they sign a **hängavtal** (attachment agreement)
- However, under Utstationeringslagen and EU posting rules, they must pay at least the rates in any generally applicable collective agreement — or negotiate directly

In practice, Sweden's regime is more complex than countries with statutory minimums — foreign carriers should seek advice from the Swedish Transport Employers' Association.

## Enforcement and Penalties

| Violation | Potential consequence |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Failure to register posting | Fine in host member state |
| Not paying minimum wage | Recovery of unpaid wages + fines |
| Insufficient documentation in cab | Fine, vehicle immobilisation |
| Exceeding cabotage limits | Fine, transport ban |

**Swedish enforcement:** Arbetsmiljöverket (work environment authority) and Polismyndigheten.
